1. Charging Model Change

In 2024/2025, the charging model shifted from per-leg billing to an all-in parent structure. This is the single biggest reason headline numbers appear inflated. All Woop volume is now charged on the "Woop Ltd" account (WOOP client code).

Old Model (rel=1)

Each leg charged separately. A delivery job = local delivery only. Linehaul and pickup were separate jobs or covered by Woop.

$8.75 delivery leg only (2017 base)

New Model (rel=19 parent)

Parent = all-in price (pickup + linehaul + delivery). Children (rel=20) = breakdown legs.

$14.04 includes linehaul (Feb 2026)

Key point: The ~$14 parent price includes linehaul (~$2–4/box) that was previously invisible in per-box pricing. Comparing $14 to historical delivery-leg prices is not apples-to-apples.

What Inflates the Headline Number

If Woop is looking at total invoiced cost ÷ total boxes nationally, the headline number will be inflated by:

  1. Linehaul now bundled in parent price — ~$2–4 per box that was previously separate/invisible
  2. South Island & regional expansion — 10–15% of volume at 20–300% premium over Auckland metro
  3. Fuel surcharges — $0.68–1.05 per box (not included in "ex fuel" figures)

6. Run Density & Volume

Conclusion

Through Feb 2026, Urgent Couriers' delivery-leg pricing has tracked at CPI since 2017 (+31% vs CPI ~28%). The three factors that create the appearance of steeper increases are:

  1. Charging model change — bundling linehaul (~$2–4/box) into a parent price inflates the headline number to ~$14
  2. Geographic expansion — shift from 79% Auckland to ~60%, adding South Island and regional sites at 20–250% premium
  3. Volume-driven density loss — drops/run halved from peak, mechanically raising per-unit cost

On a like-for-like basis (delivery leg only, ex fuel), pricing at $11.49 in 2026 reflects genuine cost-to-serve and remains fair, competitive, and essentially at inflation.
