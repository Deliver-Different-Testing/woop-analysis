Data Methodology & Corrections

✅ Deduplication applied: Data is sourced from two databases — Railway PG (Jul 2015 – Nov 2025) and AWS RDS (Apr 2025 – Mar 2026). The overlap period (Apr – Nov 2025) contained 132,855 duplicate ucjbIDs. All figures in this report are deduplicated by ucjbID. Total unique records: 1,090,224.
✅ New model filtering (post-April 2025): From April 2025, each Woop order creates multiple job records — a parent plus child legs suffixed DEL (delivery), LHP (linehaul pickup), and LH (linehaul). For apples-to-apples comparison with pre-2025 delivery pricing, all post-April 2025 figures use DEL-suffix jobs only. LH and LHP legs are excluded.
✅ Outlier removal: 44 records in 2018 with amounts >$200 (data quality issues, avg ~$1.8M) have been excluded. All years filtered to amounts >$0 and <$200.

Executive Summary

Delivery-leg pricing has tracked NZ CPI since 2017. The corrected 2025 delivery-leg price (DEL jobs only, ex fuel) of $11.58 is +34% above 2017 — tracking CPI of ~27%. The headline "all-in" parent price (~$12–14) reflects a charging model change that bundles linehaul into the order — not a rate increase above what cost-to-serve demands.

Analysis across all five regional cities (Hamilton/Tauranga, Wellington, Christchurch, Dunedin, Queenstown) confirms metro zone rates remain $8–9.50 everywhere. Average price increases are driven by volume collapse and geographic mix shift to outer zones — not unit rate inflation.

1. Charging Model Change

In April 2025, the charging model shifted from per-leg billing to an all-in parent structure. This is the single biggest reason headline numbers appear inflated.

Old Model (pre-April 2025)

Each leg charged separately. A delivery job = local delivery only. Linehaul and pickup were separate jobs or covered by Woop.

$8.62 delivery leg (2017 base)

New Model (April 2025+)

Parent = all-in price. Children suffixed DEL (delivery), LHP (pickup), LH (linehaul). For comparison we use DEL-suffix only.

$11.58 DEL delivery leg, ex fuel (2025)

Key point: The parent price includes linehaul (~$2–4/box) that was previously invisible in per-box pricing. Looking at total invoice ÷ total boxes without filtering to DEL-suffix jobs mixes in LH/LHP legs at ~$5–6, which artificially deflates the apparent delivery price to ~$9.43. The true delivery-leg comparison is the DEL-suffix price of $11.58.

Queenstown

Essentially abandoned: Only 19 jobs total across all years. Queenstown was trialled but abandoned due to extreme cost — remote geography, no local driver density, and no viable route economics. This is evidence that Urgent does not force unprofitable routes; when a market doesn't work, it gets dropped.

Conclusion

Delivery-leg pricing has risen +34% since 2017, tracking close to CPI of ~27%. Three structural factors drive cost increases beyond pure inflation:

  1. Charging model change (April 2025) — bundling linehaul and pickup into parent jobs creates child records (LH, LHP) that, if counted, artificially deflate the average to ~$9. The true delivery-leg (DEL) price is $11.58
  2. Geographic expansion — shift from 79% Auckland to ~60%, adding South Island and regional sites at 20–250% premium
  3. Volume-driven density loss — drops/run halved from 30 (peak) to 18, mechanically raising per-unit cost

Multi-city evidence confirms the pattern: Metro zone rates remain $8–9.50 in every city. Hamilton/Tauranga metro suburbs (Mt Maunganui $9.25, Bethlehem $8.98) sit at Z1 while Cambridge ($20.89) and Rotorua ($19.14) at Z4-5 inflate the average. Wellington — 83% metro with minimal mix shift — is the cleanest proof that density loss alone drives pricing up. Christchurch shows suburban sprawl (Z1 dropping 80%→75%) as satellite towns grow. Dunedin (2,400 lifetime jobs at $13–20) and Queenstown (19 total jobs, abandoned) prove geographic expansion lifts the national average without benefiting cost-to-serve.

On a corrected, deduplicated, like-for-like basis (DEL delivery legs only, ex fuel), pricing is consistent with cost-to-serve increases. The monthly data shows stable delivery pricing at $11.89–$12.83 with no sudden spikes or unexplained jumps.
