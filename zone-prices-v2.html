Methodology (v2 — corrected):
  • Base rate = RawBaseAmount field from tucJobArchive (ex fuel, ex GST)
  • Zone classification uses postcode-based assignment — each delivery postcode maps to a zone based on distance from city centre, replacing the ratio-based method used in v1
  • Fuel correction: Base rates exclude fuel surcharge. Pre-2021 no fuel was charged to Woop
  • Parent/child filtering: Only DEL-suffix children counted for RDS data (Apr 2025+), preventing double-counting of parent+child jobs
  • 6 zones now tracked: Z1 (Central), Z2 (Inner), Z3 (Mid), Z4 (Outer), Z5 (Extended), Z6 (Rural)
  • Data sources: Railway PG (historical through Mar 2025) + RDS (Apr 2025 onwards)
  • Auckland only, Sundays only, Speed 95 (Home Delivery). Cap $200 to exclude data errors

Key observations:
  • Z1 base rate below CPI: +22.8% vs ~28% NZ CPI (2017→2025). Base delivery rates have been held back
  • Z2 now tracked from 2017: +35.5% increase, above CPI — postcode classification fixed the data gap from v1
  • Z4 below CPI: +26.6% (2017→2025) vs 28% CPI — outer zone rates also held back
  • Z5/Z6 volatile: Small volumes make these zones noisy. Z5 +96.2%, Z6 +48.5% but from very low base volumes
  • Volume peaked 2021: Total Sunday volume peaked at ~122K deliveries in 2021, declining to ~25K in 2025
  • Zone mix shift: Z1 share dropped as Woop expanded range, pushing weighted average price up even as individual zone rates tracked near or below CPI
