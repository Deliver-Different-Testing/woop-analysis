Key observations:
  • Z1 dipped 8.8% during 2017–2020 (volume surge from 23K → 109K jobs drove density discounts), then jumped 59.5% from 2020–2025 as volume fell back to 54K
  • All zones exceed CPI — the gap is primarily fuel surcharge (FAF), which is baked into job prices but not in the ex-fuel rate card
  • Zone mix shifted dramatically — Z1 went from 93% of volume (2017) to 34% (2025) as Woop expanded delivery range into outer suburbs
  • Z4 shows the smallest increase (+36% since 2022) — outer zone pricing was already higher, less room for density-driven increases
  • 2017–2018 had effectively only Z1 volume (93%+); Z2–Z4 only show meaningful data from 2019
