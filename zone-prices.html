Methodology:
  • Base rate = RawBaseAmount field from tucJobArchive (ex fuel, ex GST). Pre-2021 this equals the total charged amount (no fuel surcharge applied to Woop)
  • Zone classification uses ratio boundaries from 2025 rate card (Z1=$8.81, Z2=$9.25, Z3=$9.70, Z4=$11.33), scaled to each year's Z1 anchor price
  • 2025 base rates match rate card almost exactly: Z1 $8.76≈$8.81, Z2 $9.25=$9.25, Z3 $9.74≈$9.70
  • Data sources: Railway PG (historical through Mar 2025) + RDS (Apr 2025 onwards, DEL-suffix children only)
  • Auckland only, Sundays only, Speed 95 (Home Delivery) + Speed 4 (3 Hour, pre-2019). Cap $200 to exclude data errors

Key observations:
  • Z1 base rate tracks CPI: +28.1% vs ~28% NZ CPI (2017→2025). The "above CPI" increase that Woop perceives is almost entirely fuel surcharge
  • Z1 dipped during COVID volume surge: $6.84 → $5.82 (2020, -15%) when volume hit 109K jobs/year, then recovered as volume normalised
  • Fuel surcharge adds 12-14% on top of base rate (visible from 2022 onwards). Pre-2021 Woop was not charged fuel
  • Zone mix is the biggest driver of average price increase: Z1 share dropped from 93% (2017) to 34% (2025) as Woop expanded delivery range
  • Z4 base rate actually below CPI: +15% (2017→2025) vs 28% CPI — outer zone rates have been held back
