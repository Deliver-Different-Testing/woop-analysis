Woop Cost Decomposition

What's driving per-box cost increases from 2017 to 2025 • Generated 2026-04-11

The Bottom Line

Woop's average all-in per-box cost has increased from $8.45 in 2017 to $12.10 in 2024 (+$3.65, +43%) and $14.73 in 2025 (+$6.28, +74%). The 2025 jump is primarily driven by linehaul now being charged as a visible per-box component ($2.31/box) rather than being bundled into other costs. Base delivery rate inflation accounts for the majority of the 2017–2024 increase.

Linehaul Cost Evolution

Linehaul (the cost of transporting boxes from depot to delivery areas) has been handled in three distinct periods.

Period 1 — Pre-2023: Linehaul was not charged as a separate line item. It was either bundled into base rates or absorbed as an operational cost. No separate linehaul jobs appear in the data before September 2023.
Period 2 — Sep 2023 to Feb 2025: Linehaul began appearing as separate jobs (LH/LHP suffix) but volumes were minimal — only 8 jobs in 2023 and 219 in 2024, contributing just $0.04/box in 2024.
Period 3 — March 2025 onwards (new per-box model): Linehaul is now built into the per-box price structure. 62,093 linehaul jobs in 2025, contributing $2.31 per box to the all-in cost. This is now a significant and visible cost component.

Geographic Expansion

Woop's geographic footprint has expanded from Auckland-focused operations to national coverage. This section uses 2019+ data when regional run name classification became reliable.

Impact on average cost: Geographic expansion has a moderating effect on the weighted average cost. Auckland (the lowest-cost region at ~$11.70/box in 2025) consistently represents ~60% of volume. Wellington costs are similar to Auckland, while Waikato/BoP and Christchurch have higher per-box costs due to longer distances and linehaul requirements.

Methodology & Data Notes

  • Data source: tucjobarchive table, filtered to client codes WOOP, WOOPH, WOOPW, WOOPC. Non-void jobs only.
  • Delivery classification: Old model (pre-2025): rel=1, speed IN (95, 94, 4, 120, 110, 96). New model (2025+): rel=20, speed=95 (DEL suffix).
  • Linehaul classification: rel=20, speed IN (142, 126) — LHP and LH1 child jobs in the new per-box model.
  • Parent jobs (rel=19) excluded to avoid double-counting with DEL child jobs.
  • Region classification: Based on run name prefix (AW=Auckland, HW/TW=Waikato/BoP, WW=Wellington, CW=Christchurch). Pre-2019 data used a different naming convention without run names, so appears as "Other".
  • Base rate vs fuel split: rawbaseamount and fuelsurchargeamount fields available from ~2021. Earlier years show total amount as base.
  • Linehaul per box: Total linehaul revenue for a period divided by number of delivery jobs in the same period.
  • All-in cost: Delivery amount + linehaul per box. This is the true cost per box to the customer.
  • Amount filter: Jobs with amount ≤$0 or >$500 excluded as outliers (2,097 records).
  • Total records analysed: 1,190,015 rows; 1,018,640 classified as deliveries; 62,320 as linehaul.
