# Woop Delivery Pricing Analysis
## Briefing for Steve Bonnici, CEO — Urgent Couriers

---

## Executive Summary

Delivery-leg pricing has **tracked at or below NZ CPI since 2017**. The delivery leg price increased +23% vs CPI of ~27%. The headline "all-in" figure of $14.01 reflects a charging model change that bundles linehaul — not a rate increase.

---

## 1. Charging Model Change (Key Context)

**Old model (rel=1):** Each leg charged separately. Delivery job = local delivery only. Linehaul and pickup were separate.

**New model (rel=19):** Parent = all-in price (pickup + linehaul + delivery) at $14.01. Children (rel=20) = breakdown legs.

**The $14.01 includes linehaul that was previously invisible.** The comparable delivery-leg-only figure is $11.47 (+23% vs 2017).

---

## 3. Auckland Deep Dive

### Zone Mix Stability
Auckland zone distribution has remained stable (Z1 Inner: 41.2% → 39.9%), confirming mix shift is not driving price increases within Auckland.

### Same-Route Benchmark: Depot → Remuera
Isolating a single unchanged route: price went from $7.00 (2017) to $9.12 (2024) = +30%. But volume on this route dropped from 6,090 (2021) to 2,570 (2024) — a 58% decline. Fewer drops per run = higher cost per delivery.

---

## 4. Geographic Expansion Impact

Auckland's share dropped from 79.3% (2017) to 59.5% (2024). Higher-cost regions now represent 40% of volume:

- Auckland: $10.71 | Wellington: $10.61 | Christchurch: $11.27
- Tauranga: $12.14 | Hamilton: $12.65 | Hawkes Bay: $13.53
- Remote sites: Rotorua $23.37, Coromandel $36.97, Waiheke $32.89

**Christchurch went from 0% to ~10% of national volume from 2019.** This alone lifts the national average without any rate increase.

---

## 5. Run Density Collapse

| Year | Avg Drops/Run | Volume |
|------|-------------|--------|
| 2020 | 29.8 | 195K (COVID peak) |
| 2021 | 28.8 | 223K (peak) |
| 2024 | 17.7 | 82K |

Drops per run halved from peak. With fewer parcels per run, the fixed cost of each run is spread across fewer deliveries, mechanically raising the per-unit cost.

---

## Conclusion

Pricing is fair and reflects genuine cost-to-serve. Three structural factors explain apparent increases:

1. **Charging model change** — linehaul bundled into parent price inflates headline number
2. **Geographic expansion** — shift from 79% Auckland to 60%, adding higher-cost regions
3. **Volume-driven density loss** — drops/run halved from peak, raising per-unit cost

On a like-for-like delivery-leg basis, pricing has tracked **below NZ CPI** since 2017.
